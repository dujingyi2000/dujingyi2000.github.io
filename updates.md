About Section — Employment-oriented English Version
Jingyi (Samantha) Du is a musicology-trained cultural content strategist, researcher, and project practitioner working across digital music, sound studies, international arts programming, and cross-cultural media. She is currently pursuing an MScR in Musicology at the University of Edinburgh, supported by the Pamela Jackson Memorial Scholarship. Her current research, in collaboration with St Cecilia’s Hall, examines silent instruments, proxy sound, museum interpretation, and public listening through organology, Chinese listening philosophy, phenomenology, and media theory.
Alongside academic research, she has built professional experience in international arts programming, music journalism, public relations, editorial work, publishing, and orchestral leadership. Her work has involved multilingual content coordination, project documentation, public-facing cultural communication, stakeholder liaison, music and arts writing, campaign support, user-oriented interpretation, and cross-institutional collaboration.
Her current professional interests lie in digital music platforms, international music content, music IP strategy, artist and content localisation, platform operations, sound curation, AI-assisted creative workflows, and the relationship between music, technology, and public experience. She aims to work at the intersection of music, culture, technology, and global media, translating complex artistic and intellectual materials into accessible, strategically useful, and culturally sensitive content.
