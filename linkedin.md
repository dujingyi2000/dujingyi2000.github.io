About
I am currently a Pamela Jackson Memorial Scholar pursuing an MSc by Research (MScR) in Musicology at the University of Edinburgh, working in collaboration with St Cecilia's Hall Musical Instrument Museum. I hold a BA in Chinese Language and Literature and an MPhil-equivalent MA in Comparative and World Literature from Renmin University of China. My intellectual interests sit at the intersection of aesthetics, phenomenology (especially philosophy of listening), curation theory and game studies.
 
I specialise in translating complex ideas into clear, compelling narratives—bridging scholarship, cultural insight, and public-facing communication. Alongside research,  I am also an experienced double bassist.
 
Graduating in Autumn 2026, I am seeking internationally oriented roles in arts PR, external communications, and narrative/content strategy, particularly in contexts where art meets technology and where trust, interpretation, and cultural storytelling matter.
